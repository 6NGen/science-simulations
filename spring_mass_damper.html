The spring-mass-damper is the fundamental model of mechanical engineering. Every vibrating system — cars, buildings, machines — reduces to this equation. Three forces act: the spring restores, the damper dissipates, and mass resists change. Together they determine whether a system oscillates, decays, or explodes.
