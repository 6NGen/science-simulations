In 1851, Léon Foucault suspended a 67-meter pendulum beneath the Panthéon dome. Its swing plane rotated slowly — not because of the pendulum, but because Earth itself rotated beneath it. The rotation rate depends on latitude: fastest at the poles, zero at the equator.
